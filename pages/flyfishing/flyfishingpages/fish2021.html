2021 Fishing Report

2021 was the year of big fish. Mainly from putah creek. Apart from there, I also fished the Yuba river pretty frequently. I even got out to Utah to fish the Provo river and up to Humboldt to get a coastal cutthroat. Oh, I also went steelhead fishing on the Feather river for the first and only time ever. I caught one on my first cast of the day which kind of ruined the whole concept of "fish of a thousand casts" for me. Down below you can see them.

My experience at Putah Creek began with catching a ton of short but thick fish. Its funny how short they'll be while still maintaining a healthy tummy. These guys are going to grow big. I was fishing the creek very frequently, normally 3-4 times a week. At one point, I had it down a science, and it was unusual to leave without catching 10-15 fish in a few hours. Those days are gone though as the waters change every year and I'm no longer as in touch with the creek.

As mentioned above, I made it up to Northern California and got a couple coastal cutthroat trout. Here's one of them below. Small, but a fish is a fish.

When I wasn't fishing Putah Creek, I would often hit the Yuba. The only issues with the Yuba were that it was an hour and a half away and it required us to wake up early. Regardless, we still got out there often for some decent fish.

Here are the best fish from that Provo trip I mentioned. That trip was really interesting. On one day of fishing we each caught about 14 fish each. But then on the next day, we got the skunk. I dont know what happened.

Alright, y'all have been patient to see the big ones from Putah. While I was fishing 3-4 times a week, developing a fail-safe fish catching method, I stumbled upon a couple secret techniques. I won't elaborate on them here, but lets just say they improved our fishing game immensly. After that, a large 18-20+ inch trout was nearly guarenteed on every outing. Here are a few of them.

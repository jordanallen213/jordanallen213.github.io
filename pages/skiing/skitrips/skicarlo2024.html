Mount Carlo, The Alaska Range

September 22nd, 2024

People ask me all the time on hikes, "Who mows the tundra? How does it stay so short?"

Well, a little known Denali secret is that Sam and I mow it every Sunday morning before our business meetings.

The willow and dwarf birch had been growing awfully long on Carlo so we broke out the skis for maximum mowage.

Lo and behold, the tundra got its trimming and I got some complimentry core shots.

So long Alaska until May!

Harding Ice Field, Alaska

August 9th, 2024

On our weekend, Dylan, Spenny, Iona, and I drove down to Seward from Denali to get some skiing (and snowboarding) in. Spenny and Dylan needed the month of August to complete their “year of snowboarding” challenge. We stopped in Anchorage for the night to wait out a storm and to hit the museum.

In the morning we finished the drive to Seward and browsed the town for a little. Spenny and I had $3 mystery shots for lunch to fuel up. After that we drove to the Harding Ice Field and Exit Glacier.

On the hike up to the glacier we got all sorts of awesome comments from other hikers.

“That looks like fun!”

“Are you going to ski on the glacier!?”

“I'm not even going to ask...”

“Where can I watch this like what is that on? Youtube?”
“Grindr.”

On the way down someone even asked us to go back up so they could get a video.

Once at the top we picked a few cool perennial snow fields and went to town. We each got a couple laps in before the weather came in and it started to rain.

Overall the snow was pretty depleted and a little suncupped but very nice and soft. This place would be awesome earlier in the season.

Spenny drove us all the way home to Denali that night which was an insane feat. We got back at around 3:30 in the morning- just in time for work later that day.

This one is more of a creative piece be warned I guess.

A light breeze chased me down the West Ridge of Pyramid Peak. Gaining on me.

Hopping from talus to scree, sliding, then leaping to talus again. Carrying on west. I am coasting. Effortlessly. Manzanita twigs lunge for my ankles yet seldom catch the sole of my boots.

Why is this hitting me now? A gust of mountain wind straight to the face- I’m frozen in my tracks. It caught me.

Something is wrong with me. I don’t know what- neither do the doctors. What is going on? The mountains aren’t replying. It’s rare that they don’t have the answers.

Every week I push my limits on any climb I can find, reaching summit after summit. I’ve become accustomed to the risk involved, acknowledging that one day I might push my limits too far. It’s a slap in the face that I’m stuck at home when my body decides to do me in.

For months, the hallucinations have been getting stronger- more frequent. Three times this week alone I’ve fought to keep myself from passing out in the middle of downtown. Eventually I landed myself in the ER. Things are getting worse.

Now, I'm laying in the dirt, gasping for air. Not from altitude. Tears charge down my face.

Sand is harder to grasp with wind-chapped hands.

Five sixths of my brain is screaming at me to get up but my body can’t respond. In a view from above, I see myself sitting in my backyard, in a wheelchair, struggling to push it into the pool.

Only the mountains can do this to me. Time and time again they call me over, force the truth upon me, then pat me on the back as I storm out the door in rage. Deep down I know that they’re just looking out for me.

This is what gives me a sense of accomplishment. I just submitted Pyramid Peak in record time (my PR) and was on track to cut my car 2 car record time in half. I’m in great shape right now, I better use it while I am still able to. That's my motivation at the moment. That’s what I'm holding on to.

The sun’s setting now, I guess I’ll keep going, I’ll be faster in the dark.
